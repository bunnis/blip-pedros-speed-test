Download How fast your connection pulls data from this server, it matters for loading pages, streaming and downloads. We download files of increasing size over several parallel connections. The headline is the 90th-percentile speed: sort every sample and take the point 90% of them fall at or below, which is close to your best sustained speed, ignoring brief dips and the slow start at the beginning of each transfer.

Upload How fast your connection pushes data to this server, it matters for video calls, backups and sending files. Measured over several parallel connections. The headline is the 90th-percentile speed: sort every sample and take the point 90% of them fall at or below, which is close to your best sustained speed rather than the peak or the average.
-Mbps
Latency Round-trip time: how long a small request takes to reach the server and come back, measured while the line is idle. Lower is better, it's what makes a connection feel responsive. We report the median of many samples.
-ms
Jitter How much the round-trip time varies from one request to the next. Even with low latency, high jitter disrupts calls and games. Calculated as the average change between consecutive measurements; lower is better.
-ms
Packet Loss Estimated. A browser can't see true packet loss since it happens below HTTP, and TCP silently re-sends dropped packets before your code notices. Instead we send a burst of tiny requests and count how many don't reply within a short window. For a live call or game, a reply that arrives too late is as good as lost, so this is a practical "is my connection dropping or stalling?" proxy, not a true network-layer figure. The window is scaled to your own baseline latency so a slow link isn't unfairly penalised. Lower is better.
